Potential Effects of a Tax on Robots

  • Slower adoption of automated systems
  • Fewer layoffs from robots taking jobs
  • Government revenue to apply towards paying laborers to perform more personable jobs
  • Government could reinvest money back into business

Although production has increased over the years, the number of people employed in manufacturing has been on a constant decline. Given an average blue collar salary of $31,400 and a corporate income tax of 15%, the lost revenue from income tax was $25,000,000,000 in 2010 given 5.6 million at $4,400 a person each year.

RoboTax

Website to estimate the required yearly tax on an automated system in order to ensure that government income tax does not drop when a company changes to an automated system

Old employee yearly taxes divided by productivity is equal to taxes on Automation and New hires divided by productivity of the Automation

  • Government can determine potential for tax revenue
  • Businesses can calculate how much they would have to spend in taxes
  • Businesses may use to assist in making investment decisions (automate or pay for labor)
  • Legal and Finance sectors can make more informed decisions on future investments and liabilities
