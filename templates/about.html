About

“If a human does $50,000 worth of work in a factory, that income is taxed. If a robot comes in to do the same, you’d think we’d tax the robot at a similar level”
- Bill Gates

Although production has increased over the years, the number of people employed in manufacturing has been on a constant decline. Given an average blue collar salary of $31,400 and a corporate income tax of 15%, the lost revenue from income tax was $25,000,000,000 in 2010 given 5.6 million at $4,400 per person each year.

RoboTax

RoboTax estimates a required yearly tax on an automated system given information on replaced employees. The estimation is designed to raise tax revenue and compensate for lower income tax as robots and algorithms replace workers.

Old employee yearly taxes divided by productivity is equal to taxes on automation and new hires divided by productivity of the automation.

Potential Effects of a Tax on Robots

  • Slower adoption of automated systems.
  • Fewer layoffs from robots taking jobs.
  • Government could reinvest money back into business.
  • Government revenue increase applies towards paying laborers to perform more personable jobs.
  • Government can determine potential for tax revenue.
  • Businesses can calculate how much they would have to spend in taxes.
  • Businesses may use to assist in making investment decisions (automate or pay for labor).
  • Legal and Finance sectors can make more informed decisions on future investments and liabilities.
